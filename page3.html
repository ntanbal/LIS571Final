Privacy issues raise ethical concerns regarding data protection. Individuals must be informed about how their personal information will be collected, used, and shared, with informed consent being crucial for transparency and trust. Despite few laws in the United States safeguarding consumer data, California has enacted comprehensive legislation, leading in data protection. As technology introduces new ethical dilemmas, companies must ensure fair data protection policies, including secure data management practices such as encryption and regular security updates. Sharing data with other entities should require explicit permission.
